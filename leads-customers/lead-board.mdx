Your Lead Board is where you track every person who calls your business. Think of it as a visual pipeline showing exactly where each potential customer stands — from the moment your AI receptionist captures their details to the moment you win (or lose) the job.

## Board View vs Table View

You have two ways to look at your leads. Switch between them using the toggle at the top of the page.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Board View" icon="columns-3">
    Drag-and-drop columns. Perfect for a quick visual overview of your pipeline. Great on desktop.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Table View" icon="table">
    Searchable, sortable list. Better for finding specific leads or working through a long list. Great on mobile.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The Five Columns

Every lead sits in one of five columns. Drag a lead card from one column to the next as you work with them.

| Column | What It Means | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **New** | Just came in. You haven't looked at it yet. | Someone called about a boiler repair 10 minutes ago. |
| **In Progress** | You've made contact or sent a quote. | You called them back and discussed the job. |
| **Qualified** | They're serious and ready to book. | They've agreed on a price and want to schedule. |
| **Won** | Job booked or completed. Money in the bank. | Appointment confirmed, deposit paid. |
| **Lost** | They went elsewhere or stopped responding. | No reply after 3 follow-ups. |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Drag to move a lead">
    Click and hold any lead card, then drag it to the column that matches its current status. The change saves automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click for details">
    Click any lead card to open the full detail view — call recording, notes, contact info, and quick actions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Temperature Badges

Your AI receptionist scores every lead automatically based on the call. You'll see a coloured badge on each lead card.

| Badge | Temperature | What It Means |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| 🔴 **HOT** | 80-100 | Ready to buy now. Call them back immediately. |
| 🟠 **WARM** | 60-79 | Interested but needs a nudge. Follow up today. |
| 🟡 **COOL** | 40-59 | Might be shopping around. Worth a follow-up this week. |
| 🔵 **COLD** | 0-39 | Low intent or just asking questions. Check in later. |

<Tip>
Focus your time on HOT and WARM leads first. These are the people most likely to book right now. A quick callback within 5 minutes can double your conversion rate.
</Tip>

## How Lead Scoring Works

The AI looks at 7 factors from the call to calculate each lead's score:

1. **Urgency** — Did they say "emergency," "ASAP," or "today"? Higher urgency = higher score.
2. **Service match** — Does the service they need match what you offer?
3. **Budget signals** — Did they ask about pricing without flinching, or are they price-shopping?
4. **Timeline** — Do they want something done this week or "sometime next year"?
5. **Decision authority** — Are they the homeowner/decision-maker, or calling on someone else's behalf?
6. **Contact completeness** — Did they provide their name, phone, email, and address?
7. **Engagement** — How long was the call? Did they ask detailed questions?

<Info>
Lead scores update automatically. You never need to calculate anything yourself. The AI does it all during the call.
</Info>

## Using Table View

Switch to Table View when you need to:

- **Search** — Type a name, phone number, or service into the search bar to find a specific lead instantly.
- **Sort** — Click any column header (Name, Date, Temperature, Status) to sort ascending or descending.
- **Filter** — Use the status dropdown to show only New leads, only HOT leads, or any combination.
- **Bulk actions** — Select multiple leads using the checkboxes to move them all to a new status at once.

## Tips for Managing Your Board

<Accordion title="How often should I check my lead board?">
  At minimum, check it every morning and every evening. HOT leads should be contacted within 5 minutes if possible — businesses that call back within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to win the job.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if a lead calls back again?">
  The AI recognises returning callers automatically. Their existing lead card gets updated with the new call details rather than creating a duplicate.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I add leads manually?">
  Yes. Click the **+ New Lead** button at the top of the board to create a lead by hand — useful for walk-ins, website enquiries, or referrals that didn't come through a phone call.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I delete a lead?">
  Open the lead detail panel and click **Delete** at the bottom. This is permanent and cannot be undone. If you just lost the job, move the lead to the **Lost** column instead — that way you keep the history.
</Accordion>
